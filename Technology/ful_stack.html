FAQ

Full Stack Development — Common Questions

Straight answers to the questions every technical founder and engineering leader asks us.

What makes MCKBytes different from a typical development agency?

We operate on a senior-only model — every engagement is led by engineers who have shipped production systems at scale, not delegated to juniors. We also maintain a flat, accountable team structure where the people you meet in scoping are the people who write your code.

Can you work with our existing codebase and internal team?

Absolutely — in fact, most of our engagements are embedded team models. We integrate with your existing workflows, code standards, and tools. We can lead the technical work, augment your team, or work in a consulting capacity depending on what you need.

How do you handle IP and code ownership?

All code, documentation, and design assets created for your project are fully transferred to you upon final payment. We sign a standard IP assignment agreement at the start of every engagement. Your product is yours — completely.

What's your quality assurance and testing process?

Every project includes unit tests, integration tests, and end-to-end tests as part of the standard delivery — not as optional extras. We enforce test coverage thresholds in CI, run performance benchmarks before releases, and conduct security reviews prior to launch.

Do you offer post-launch support and maintenance?

Yes — we offer flexible retainer arrangements for post-launch support, monitoring, performance tuning, feature additions, and on-call incident response. Many clients stay with us for years after the initial project as a trusted engineering partner.

How long does a typical full stack project take?

An MVP typically takes 8–14 weeks. A full product rebuild or complex SaaS platform typically runs 16–26 weeks. We provide a detailed timeline breakdown during the architecture phase, with milestone checkpoints every two weeks.

Can you help us scale our architecture as we grow?

Scaling is built into our architecture decisions from day one — we don't design for current load, we design for 10× current load. When you need to scale further, we can redesign hot paths, introduce caching layers, shard databases, or migrate to a distributed architecture as part of an ongoing engagement.

Do you work with startups or only enterprise clients?

Both — and we genuinely enjoy both. Startups benefit from our speed, pragmatism, and ability to make smart architectural trade-offs under constraint. Enterprise clients benefit from our process rigour, security posture, and ability to navigate complex existing systems. The standards are the same either way.
