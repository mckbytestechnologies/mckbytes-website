FAQ

Full Stack Development — Common Questions

Straight answers to the questions every technical founder and engineering leader asks us.

What makes mckbytes different from a typical development agency?

We operate on a senior-only model — every engagement is led by engineers who have shipped production systems at scale, not delegated to juniors. The people you meet in scoping are the people who write your code. We focus on quality, efficiency, and innovation.

Can you work with our existing codebase and team?

Yes — most of our engagements are embedded-team models. We integrate with your workflows, code standards and tools, and can lead the work, augment your team, or consult depending on what you need.

How do you handle IP and code ownership?

All code, documentation and design assets are transferred to you upon final payment. We sign a standard IP assignment agreement at the start of every engagement.

What's your quality assurance and testing process?

Unit, integration and end-to-end tests are part of standard delivery — not optional extras. We enforce coverage thresholds in CI, run performance benchmarks before releases, and conduct security reviews prior to launch.

Do you offer post-launch support and maintenance?

Yes — flexible retainers for post-launch support, monitoring, performance tuning, feature additions and on-call incident response. Many clients stay with us for years after the initial project.

How long does a typical full stack project take?

An MVP typically takes 8–14 weeks. A full product rebuild or complex SaaS platform usually runs 16–26 weeks. We provide a detailed timeline during architecture, with milestone checkpoints every two weeks.

Do you work with startups or only enterprise clients?

Both — and we genuinely enjoy both. Startups benefit from our speed, pragmatism and architectural trade-offs under constraint. Enterprise clients benefit from our process rigour, security posture and ability to navigate complex existing systems.

What technologies do you work with?

Front-End: React.js, Angular, Vue.js, HTML5, CSS3, Bootstrap. Back-End: Node.js, Python (Django, Flask), Ruby on Rails, PHP, Java. Databases: MySQL, PostgreSQL, MongoDB, Firebase. Mobile: React Native, Flutter, Swift, Kotlin. Cloud: AWS, Azure, Google Cloud, Docker, Jenkins.
